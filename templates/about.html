About

A little more information about how Schemes SG came to be and thinking behind it.

Our vision

Our vision is to empower social workers, volunteers, and in the long run even self-help users, to navigate and obtain relevant information on social assistance in Singapore quickly, easily and accurately. We tap on the power of crowdsourcing to keep information comprehensive and updated. We then leverage technology to make this information more navigable.

How it started

Schemes SG started as a small side project to put up a list of assistance schemes -- collated from product lead Weilie's years of volunteering -- in the form of a mobile app, and later a web app prototype, for a polytechnic programming assignment. The list was compiled to address his own pain point of not being able to find social assistance information easily.

The sudden interest by friends and strangers in the app MVP took him by surprise, and he realised many other people faced the same pain point. After conducting user research, and understanding how care workers think about the assistance landscape, the original app was improved with a tag system to organise the information. Soon, an AI-driven natural language search feature was also added.

In March 2021, after establishing that this indeed plugged a social sector gap, Schemes SG became a larger initiative when it tapped on the collective energy and passion of technologists and social sector volunteers from better.sg.

What this platform tries to solve

Very serious thought was put in before investing effort to build Schemes SG. Landscape scan uncovered the following issues:

1. Social assistance listings are piecemeal and fragmented across the internet. There are some compilations but they are often PDF files hidden deep within organisations' file repositories, so they may not be easy to find or picked up by search engines.

2. Even if one could find a compilation, no one will Ctrl + F all of them to find schemes given how complex and multifaceted social assistance is. Even if one does, a simple string mismatch ("low income" vs "low-income") will yield a null result. One can Google, but Google may not necessarily focus on Singapore or on schemes information.

3. The listings may not necessarily be updated. New versions are usually held in completely different links. PDF listings also mean that care workers need to depend on the original poster to issue a new version.

4. There are some intuitive directories (e.g. SupportGoWhere has done a great job) but these are mainly government portals and may not include NGO or VWO schemes. Again, the power of the crowdsourcing could be useful here.

This portal hopes to address the above issues by tapping on crowd power to make social assistance info 1) comprehensive and 2) updated, and then using technologies like AI/NLP and data filters to make this info 3) navigable. 😊

Considerations behind the listings

Here are the parameters behind how the Bank was populated:

1. All information is public-domain. Where individual schemes are concerned, we think the best descriptions come from the organisations themselves and use their words wherever possible to avoid misrepresenting their good work :) If we make edits, it is to improve search functionality.

2. Currently, Schemes SG only lists schemes that provide benefits in cash (financial assistance, subsidies) or in kind (free food, food vouchers, free clinics, special cards which ascribe certain benefits). We are starting to include services (e.g. subsidised special education) but the Schemes Bank is far from complete.

Schemes SG does not include:

1. Auto-inclusion schemes. The purpose of a consolidated portal is to reduce bandwidth tax, so we see no need to put up information that social workers and volunteers have no requirement to act on.

2. Schemes that do not have a public listing, or are not verified. We understand that sometimes, organisations may have reasons for keeping their social assistance informal. If there is no public info on it, we will not include it. If the info is crowdsourced, we ask the contributor for a link, and if there is none, we will contact the organisation or even the private individual to verify first.

Technical details for geeks

Schemes Pal's natural language model involves the following transformation: Bag of Words (BoW) -> TF-IDF -> latent semantic indexing (LSI). Some resources that I used include this, this and this. I am still experimenting if this is the most appropriate for our use case. If you have any ideas on model improvement, tuning or other aspects that could help users get more accurate listings, reach out via the "Feedback" form.

The envisioned technical steady-state is a virtuous data cycle. As the user base grows, we get more Schemes Bank contributions and Schemes Pal queries, allowing us to train more robust and accurate semantic matches. Schemes Case requests cover the blind spots from a domain perspective and act as a sanity check.

Schemes SG is a product made by a team who cares about the social sector and wants to help those in need access assistance more quickly. We hope you find it helpful!
